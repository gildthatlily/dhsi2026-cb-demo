## in my work, i generally do not measure. i *do* think about what my work cannot contain or represent or make visible [keeping in mind, of course, that visibility is a trap]. i am thinking about inception, when dominic cobb tells his projection of his deceased wife that his memory of her cannot contain all of her nuances [*but i can’t imagine you with all your complexity, all your perfection, all your imperfection*]. similarly, my work — in particular, this project — cannot possibly contain all of the archival elements to which i have access. it cannot contain all of the parts of my mother and her people, my father and his people, my pasts, my histories, my presents, and my interiorities in a way that encompasses everything there is to know. there are things, long gone, that cannot be captured. there are things, to which i do not have access, that cannot be captured. there are people, long gone, who cannot be captured. there is me, to which i sometimes have access, and i cannot be captured. i am thinking about the phrase “take a picture” and the first time, in art school, i heard someone say “make a photograph” — a shift away from the language of extraction and toward the language of craft. i am also thinking about “only a part, not the whole,” the card i pulled from *oblique strategies*. what are the ethics of navigating excess? what are the ethics of navigating excess while attending to nuance? what are the ethics of navigating excess while attending to nuance and preserving the self?
